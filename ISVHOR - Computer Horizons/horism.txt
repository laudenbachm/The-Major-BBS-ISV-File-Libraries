     Are hackers targeting your Internet site?  Do you have valuable
data, or users that may want to try breaking into your system?  If
so, you need Internet Security Monitor, a NEW program by Computerized
Horizons, the company that brought you WebLines, Modem Technician, and
other popular programs for Worldgroup and Major BBS.

     Internet Security Monitor will alert you to some simple, yet
powerful, information that a hacker could easily get about your system.
For example, there may be IP's on your network that are connected to
computers you weren't aware of (could a hacker have a PPP connection
running on your Unix box?).  Also, some of your computers could have
unusual ports open that hackers might be able to take advantage of.

     Another feature of Internet Security Monitor is that it will go
through all your open ports and run scripts to get information that
a hacker might be able to find out about your system.  Even if you
have an old Unix box still connected to your site, Internet Security
Monitor will be able to show you the information a hacker could get
from its chargen, echo, and discard ports.

      Perhaps the best feature of Internet Security Monitor is the
trap that it places for hackers.  One of the worst problems about
attacks from hackers is that if they use IP spoofing (as several common
attacks, such as SYN flooding and 'r' command authorization attacks),
the hackers can't be traced after they leave your system.  However, the
trap that the Internet Security Monitor places will likely catch the
*real* IP address of the hacker *before* they attack your system.

     Even better, the trap that Internet Security Monitor places also
includes a "decoy" that the hackers will see, and likely wish to
explore.  While they are exploring the decoy, Internet Security Monitor
runs several further trace routines.  The extra information obtained
through these traces can often give you what you need to prove the
exact identity of the hacker.

     Internet Security Monitor runs on Worldgroup and Major BBS
systems, with the Major TCP/IP Internet software.  The trap that it
sets for hackers can only work on the IP address(es) of your
Worldgroup/MajorBBS system.  However, the checks that it performs for
IP addresses, ports, and script-checking of the ports *will* work on
any system in your network, including Unix and NT computers!
